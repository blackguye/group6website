  • Impact on Families: Over thousands of families were severely affected by Typhoon Yolanda, resulting in the loss of homes and lives within days of the disaster.
  • Urgent Needs and Humanitarian Crisis: Survivors urgently required help with basic necessities
    such as food, water, and shelter, highlighting the immediate humanitarian crisis following the typhoon.
  • Long-Term Trauma: The event left a traumatic mark on survivors' lives, impacting them emotionally and psychologically, persisting as a challenging memory.
  • Strength and Resilience: Despite the devastation, survivors exhibited strong faith and bravery in the face of adversity, demonstrating resilience amidst the disaster.
  • Nature of Typhoon Yolanda: Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan) was a catastrophic event characterized
    by extremely powerful winds and waves that caused massive flooding and widespread destruction.
  • National Impact: The typhoon affected millions of people across Luzon and Visayas, with significant damage to infrastructure, homes, and essential services.
  • Immediate Aftermath: Many areas were left without electricity and clean water, further complicating recovery efforts and exacerbating the humanitarian crisis.
  • Casualties and Displacement**: The typhoon resulted in approximately 6,700 fatalities and
    displacement of thousands of families, with many remaining temporarily sheltered post-disaster.
  • Reconstruction Challenges**: The severity of the damage necessitated a prolonged recovery period, making it challenging for affected communities to rebuild their lives and infrastructure.
